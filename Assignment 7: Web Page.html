Carolina Hurricanes

Previously the Hartford Whalers, the team relocated to Raleigh, North Carolina in 1997. When the team moved,
the franchise had to change to adapt to and represent their new location. The name was changed to
'Hurricanes' due to the fact that North Carolina is frequently hit by the storms. The mascot, Stormy, is
an ice hog, which represents North Carolina's pork industry.

In 2006, the Hurricanes defeated the Edmonton Oilers in the
Stanley Cup final. This was the first and, as of 2024, only
championship in franchise history. The team was led by
captain Rod Brind'Amour, who is now the current head coach of
the team.
